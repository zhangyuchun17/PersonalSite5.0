LGBTQ+ Bar Trends Visualizations (1977–2019)

This webpage visualizes the historical evolution of LGBTQ+ bar spaces in the United States from 1977 to 2019. It explores overall trends, changing compositions, and the sharp decline of some categories—such as lesbian bars—through five charts based on bar type and time. The data provides insight into shifting patterns of queer nightlife visibility and inclusion over time.

Data Source: Mattson, G. (2019). Are Gay Bars Closing? Using Business Listings to Infer Rates of Gay Bar Closure in the United States, 1977–2019. Socius, 5. https://doi.org/10.1177/2378023119894832

Chart 1: Total Number of LGBTQ+ Bars Over Time

This line chart illustrates the total number of LGBTQ+ bars in the U.S. from 1977 to 2019. The data shows growth in the early 1980s, peaking mid-decade, followed by a steady decline—especially after the 2000s—indicating a widespread loss of dedicated queer nightlife spaces.

Chart 2: Composition of LGBTQ+ Bars Over Time

This stacked area chart shows the shifting proportions of different bar types. Mostly/Only Men bars were dominant in earlier years, while Mixed M/F bars increased in the 1990s. Lesbian, POC, and Cruisy/Leather bars remained minor categories and declined, suggesting uneven spatial persistence.

Chart 3: Trends in LGBTQ+ Bar Types

This multi-line chart breaks down the trends of each category. It highlights how Lesbian bars experienced the steepest drop post-1987, while Mixed M/F bars rose significantly. Cruisy/Leather, POC, and Mostly/Only Men bars all declined, reflecting structural changes in LGBTQ+ spaces.

Chart 4: Bar Composition in 2019

This pie chart presents the 2019 composition of LGBTQ+ bars. The Lesbian segment is highlighted and appears notably small (1.6%), while Mixed M/F and Mostly/Only Men dominate. The visual underscores the marginal presence of lesbian-oriented nightlife in the contemporary scene.

Chart 5: Change in Bar Types (1977 vs. 2019)

This horizontal bar chart compares the counts of each bar type in 1977 and 2019. It reveals significant decline in Mostly/Only Men and Lesbian bars, while Mixed M/F increased. The comparison emphasizes how gender inclusivity has grown while exclusive spaces have diminished.
